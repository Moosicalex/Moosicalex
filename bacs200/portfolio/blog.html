Economic Implications

It's a strange and elucidating fact that people who have more wealth produce more carbon emmisions. The richer a country becomes, the more it pollutes the planet. It would seem that as an individual and/or country gains more wealth they would be able to better disconnect themselves from emmiting carbon. Yet ,as economic growth continues almost everywhere in the world it causes a, whether it be direct or indirect, effect on the planet. Part of the problem is that as people gain more wealth, they buy better things like laptops, better cars, vacations, and even things that we in the developed world might see less as discretionary and more essential like air-conditioning, better food, better houses, more clothes, etc. While these things are not directly causing the rising climate temperatures, the demand for them is leading to more climate change.

However, You cannot simply ask for economic growth to halt. For many people and their families this would mean staying poor for the rest of their lives. The economic implications of the changing climate cannot be ignored, however, they will not be easy to solve either.

But the problem still stands; If the climate continues to change it will have more drastic effects on the economy than we have ever seen. The rise in sea levels will result in the loss of millions of peoples homes, the changes to the populations of animal species could result in the loss of food and jobs of millions, among other effects.
